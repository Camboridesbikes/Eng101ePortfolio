Writer’s Journal #1

The 4 Outcomes

 

Here I am, writing the first of twenty journals for my English Composition course. This past week I’ve been priming to dive into the material. Learning how to use the edX online student interface, while coding to develop an e-portfolio website and grasping concepts of the subject of this entry: the course’s target outcomes; The goals of what I’ll take away from this course,along with a certificate.

I’m quite impressed at how clear and well-defined these ‘outcomes’ have been presented thus far.. There are four of them: rhetorical knowledge, critical thinking, composing processes and a knowledge of conventions. I have noticed that the course is using repetition of the material and various ways to convey the concepts behind the course outcomes, along with how to apply them. The outcomes were explained in videos, in a pdf, in individual lessons and even now through writing about them, I am deepening my comprehension of these outcomes by repeating and analyzing them.

Rhetorical knowledge Is the first on the list. It seems to be the one that I am having the most difficulty understanding and identifying. I like how rhetorics were explained in one of the first course videos. Dr. Duane Roen says, “ Rhetoric is all about strategies for using language to get things done in the world. And what that means is that instead of using other tools in the world, language can be a tool for getting things done, just as it would if you were using a shovel or a hammer or anything else.” This quote is what I am relying on as a reference to reach a firm grasp on the concepts of rhetorical knowledge.

Critical thinking is the next outcome of this writing course. I have a knack for critical thinking and am a great problem solver. That’s why I have a knack for fixing things as a bicycle mechanic and enjoy tasks that require attention to detail. Critical thinking in the context of writing also involves critical reading and composing. I believe that much of my writing has lacked an intentional approach that a critical eye needs.  Casual writing I feel that I do just fine, but preparing to enter back into academic writing I compare closely to my professional writing. I mentioned in my introduction on the course’s forum that writing in a professional capacity has been a bit of an overwhelming endeavor for me. I’m beginning to connect at least some of that, to the idea my critical eye is not employing these other concepts in it’s analysis and evaluation of what I’m writing. This is somewhere I certainly will be actively seeking improvement during this course.

The outcome that least surprises me is ‘processes’. The idea of having and creating adaptive processes to approach problems or projects with is sort of a theme in my life as of late. Through the course, having experience writing with the guidance of this course, my peers and instructors will probably give me a perfect stage to find and develop processes and approaches that work well for me. With this, I will remind myself to let my processes evolve and grow as my writing does.

Another aspect that will continue to grow along my path of writing is my knowledge of conventions. This is the fourth outcome and I’ve had a basic concept of this; but the way the course presents a knowledge of conventions is new, possibly even revolutionary to me. The idea that conventions ‘invoke common expectations between writers and readers’ is a completely new perspective for me. When I’ve been researching and attempting to understand how to write professional correspondences like resumes and letters to prospective employers, I haven’t taken this approach. I feel as though I have been trying to write in a format that almost mirrors my examples instead of understanding these expectations and writing to meet those. The lines between personal, professional and academic writing conventions aren’t quite clear to me yet.

I didn’t know what to expect coming into the course, but these four outcomes seem to set a standard guideline for development as a writer. Even applicable beyond this course! Stating them helps me begin to identify where I am as a writer and how much of an understanding I have of these concepts. They certainly seem to set a measurable target and being able to see where I’m going in this course, is getting me excited about the proverbial journey to that destination!

Life Is Wonderful!
